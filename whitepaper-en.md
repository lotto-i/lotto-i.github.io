# [World's First! LOTTOi](https://www.lottoi.kr) Public Verification Structure Whitepaper
*(English Version)*

---

> **Since Feb 8, 2025**  
> **LOTTOi is the world’s first lottery information platform to implement a public verification system integrated with external global platforms (Google & GitHub).**

---

## 1. Document Overview

Since February 8, 2025, [LOTTOi](https://www.lottoi.kr) has been recording user-generated numbers in third-party external systems (Google) **prior to the lottery draw**. 

This system ensures that **anyone can independently verify** whether those records were altered after the results were announced. This document is not intended to promote prediction performance; rather, it explains the **operational and verification structure**—including record creation timing, public disclosure methods, and how data integrity is maintained through external references.

---

## 2. Core Principles of the Public Verification Structure

[LOTTOi](https://www.lottoi.kr)’s system is built on the following principles to ensure "Transparency through Traceability":

- **World’s First External Attribution:** Records are stored in third-party systems before the draw, ensuring the operator cannot manipulate the timing.
- **Inability to Conceal Modifications:** All changes and their specific timestamps are logged via Google/GitHub, making it **technically impossible to hide alterations**.
- **Democratized Verification:** Verification is performed by **anyone (the public)**, not the operator.

Rather than claiming to prevent modifications through a closed system, [LOTTOi](https://www.lottoi.kr) is structurally designed so that **any modification, if it occurs, is fully exposed and traceable through global standard logs**.

---

## 3. Overall Public Verification Flow (Actual Operational Standard)

```
[Creation of a Round-Specific Google Public Document]
└ World's first real-time external recording method established since Feb 2025
└ Initially created with 'Viewer' permission; creation timestamp automatically recorded by Google
↓
[GitHub Anchoring: URL & Timestamp Log Storage]
└ Acts as an 'External Anchor' to fix the existence of the verification document
↓
[User Number Generation & System Closing]
↓
[Draw Day (Saturday, 20:00 ~ 20:35)] ★ Critical Data Logging Phase
└ Immediately after sales close, Google document permission switches to 'Editable'
└ All generated user numbers are saved in bulk to the Google public document
└ Storage time and all change history are recorded as real-time timestamps by Google
↓
[Lottery Draw (Starting from 20:35~)]
↓
[Public Post-Verification]
└ Anyone can verify data integrity by checking if the 'Last Modified Time'
precedes the draw time (20:35) via Google/GitHub logs
```

---

## 4. Role of the GitHub Log

The GitHub log serves as an **external reference anchor**. It proves that the Google document URL existed before the lottery draw.

*   **Anchor Point:** Fixes the earliest moment of the document's existence externally.
*   **Audit Trail:** GitHub records are transparently disclosed via commit history, showing when the anchor was set or updated.

---

## 5. Google Document Permission Management

- **5-1. Pre-Draw:** Read-only (Viewer) permission prevents any premature modification.
- **5-2. During Logging (Sat. 20:00~20:35):** Permission switches to editable only for bulk data entry. This transition is also logged by Google’s system.

This ensures all preparatory actions are preserved as **externally verifiable, time-based records**.


---

## 6. Standards for Modification and Change History

After the lottery draw, the editing permissions for the Google Public Document may remain active. While this technically allows for modifications by the operator or authorized parties, LOTTOi ensures full transparency through the following:

- **Logged Data:** Modification time, modified content, and the sequence of changes.
- **Verification:** All history is automatically recorded by the Google system and **cannot be concealed**. 

Therefore, anyone can independently verify whether the records were altered after the draw time (20:35), making any attempt at manipulation transparent and detectable.

---

## 7. Principles for Correcting Operational Errors (e.g., URL Typos)

In rare cases of operational errors, such as a typo in a URL, the GitHub record may be updated before the draw. Even in these instances, LOTTOi maintains full disclosure of:

- The initial recording timestamp.
- The exact time the modification occurred.
- The 'Before' and 'After' versions of the URL.

The core criteria for verification are whether the **finalized URL existed before the draw** and whether all **modifications were completed before 20:35 (KST) on Saturday**.

---

## 8. Key Elements for External Public Verification

Users can directly verify the integrity of the data through two primary channels:

*   **GitHub Repository:** Check the initial recording timestamp of the Google Document URL. Verify that the final commit was made prior to the lottery draw.
*   **Google Document:** Review the 'Version History'. Specifically, verify that the **final content change occurred before 20:35 (KST)** and that the content at that timestamp matches the recorded numbers.

---

## 9. Technical Limitations and Operational Environment Notice

Factors such as external network latency, global platform outages (Google/GitHub), or other technical issues may cause temporary delays in record creation or permission changes.

The structure of LOTTOi is not designed to claim a perfect, delay-free system; rather, it is designed so that **any occurrence of a delay itself cannot be concealed**. All actual timestamps remain in the third-party systems, allowing for post-verification of the sequence of events relative to the draw time.
